Introduction

This research aims to draw conclusions and insights from the National Survey of Children's Health (NSCH) , a survey developed and administered each year by the U.S. Census Bureau with funding from the Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA), Maternal and Child Health Bureau (MCHB). This household survey collects national- and state-level estimates of the physical and mental health of children 0–17 years old.

Data from the NSCH are used for a variety of purposes and as such a large body of reports and peer-reviewed publications are available. This analysis is limited to state-level comparisons and national estimates.

This project explores various machine learning models applied to data from the National Survey of Children's Health (NSCH). The dataset includes a wide range of variables related to children’s physical and mental health, family environment, and access to healthcare.

The primary goal is to build and compare different models for classification, regression, clustering, and dimensionality reduction to extract meaningful insights and make predictions about child health outcomes.

  • Data Cleaning and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
  • Clustering (K-means, Hierarchical)
  • Dimensionality Reduction (PCA)
  • Classification (Naive Bayes, Decision Trees, SVM, Neural Networks)
  • Regression Analysis

All models are evaluated based on accuracy, interpretability, and their relevance to public health insights.
